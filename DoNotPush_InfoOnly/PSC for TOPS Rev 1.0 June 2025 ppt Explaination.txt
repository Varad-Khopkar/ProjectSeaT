"PSC for TOPS Rev 1.0 June 2025" ppt explaination
---
The presentation explains how a vessel should prepare for a PSC inspection, what inspectors look for, why ships get detained, and what every crew member is responsible for. Every concept from the PPT must be represented naturally inside the simulator instead of as text-heavy pages.

## Overall Learning Goal

The learner should experience what it is like to prepare a vessel for a real Port State Control inspection.

The simulator should teach:

* What Port State Control is.
* Why PSC exists.
* How PSC inspections are conducted.
* What inspectors verify.
* What deficiencies lead to detention.
* How the crew should prepare.
* How different departments contribute.
* How international conventions such as SOLAS, MARPOL, Load Line and MLC influence inspections.

The learner should finish the simulator feeling confident enough to understand a real PSC inspection process.

---

# Module 1 - Introduction to PSC

Begin by introducing Port State Control.

Explain that PSC is an inspection conducted by the authorities of a foreign port to ensure visiting ships comply with international maritime regulations.

Clarify that although the Flag State has the primary responsibility for ensuring compliance, PSC acts as the second line of defence to identify unsafe or substandard vessels.

Explain the three major objectives:

* Safety of the crew
* Safety of the ship
* Protection of the marine environment

Instead of presenting paragraphs, create an engaging animated introduction showing a vessel arriving at port and being selected for inspection.

---

# Module 2 - PSC Inspection Process

Teach how an actual inspection happens.

The learner should understand the complete workflow:

Ship arrives at port

↓

PSC Officer boards vessel

↓

Documents reviewed

↓

Accommodation inspected

↓

Bridge inspected

↓

Engine room inspected

↓

Safety equipment inspected

↓

Environmental compliance verified

↓

Crew questioned

↓

Deficiencies recorded

↓

Ship cleared or detained

Represent this as an interactive inspection timeline.

# Module 3 - What PSC Inspectors Check

The PPT explains five major inspection areas.

Convert each into an interactive activity.

## Document Verification

Teach inspection of:

* Certificates
* Crew documents
* Licences
* Validity dates

The learner should identify expired or missing documents.

---

## Safety Equipment

Teach inspection of:

Life jackets

Fire extinguishers

Emergency equipment

Life-saving appliances

Emergency fire pump

Fire dampers

The learner should inspect equipment and identify defects.

---

## Crew Competency

The simulator should demonstrate that inspectors ask crew members questions.

The learner should verify whether crew understand:

Emergency procedures

Fire drills

Abandon ship procedures

Environmental procedures

Their own responsibilities

---

## Housekeeping

Explain that housekeeping reflects overall vessel management.

Show examples of:

Clean engine room

Dirty engine room

Blocked escape routes

Oil leaks

Poor storage

Improper maintenance

The learner decides whether each area is acceptable.

---

## Internal Inspections

Teach why routine inspections reduce PSC deficiencies.

The learner performs a pre-arrival inspection before the PSC officer arrives.

# Module 5 - Cooperation with PSC Inspector

The presentation emphasizes behaviour during inspections.

Build dialogue-based scenarios where the learner interacts with the PSC Officer.

Teach that crew should:

Remain honest

Never hide deficiencies

Never argue

Never mislead inspectors

Always cooperate professionally

Ensure a senior officer accompanies the inspector

Demonstrate that the ship is safe to sail

Different responses should influence inspection outcomes.

# Final Simulation

The learner should complete a realistic PSC inspection from start to finish.

A PSC Officer arrives unexpectedly.

The learner must:

Inspect the vessel

Identify deficiencies

Correct problems

Answer inspector questions

Present documents

Verify crew readiness

Demonstrate compliance

Receive inspection results.

The outcome should depend entirely on the learner's decisions.

Possible endings include:

Clean inspection

Minor deficiencies

Major deficiencies

Detention

---

## Most Important Requirement

Do **not** recreate the PowerPoint.

Use it as the knowledge base behind the simulator.

The learner should feel like they are walking through a real vessel preparing for a PSC inspection rather than reading slides. Every concept from the PPT should be represented through gameplay, interaction, decision-making, inspections, scenarios, quizzes, and practical shipboard experiences, making the training as close as possible to a real Port State Control Audit.
